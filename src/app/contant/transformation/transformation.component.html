Do Service Operations need to be transformed?


According to a study of responses from the most successful service organizations based on results achieved, 71% of this group had a better grasp of the definition of digital transformation services.

Customers' attitude plays a vital role in brand image and referral sales in this age of digital media and social media. Therefore, highly successful organizations take services very seriously to ensure their customers are satisfied throughout the relationship. This results in customers returning to the company again and again, as well as recommending or endorsing its products and services.

Integration of processes through digitalization

To provide a unified view of customer value, the proper service programs must be created and integrated with Marketing and Sales. Seamless integration at all 'points of customer interaction' enables Services to anticipate what to expect before or right after a sale.

Develop a fully integrated logistical and delivery value chain

Digital acceleration requires a minimum number of control points in processes necessary for a highly automated world. Suppose your business requires third-party providers, spare parts, and logistics. In that case, you must integrate your operations with those departments as highly as possible so that service operations play little or no role in service delivery. Furthermore, integrating marketing and sales automatically keeps everyone updated on the overall customer status, improving customer engagements.

Utilize Service Analytics to reduce customer interactions

While it may seem counterintuitive, great service organizations want to be 'all is well' organizations. Their commitment to analytics implies that they invest heavily in understanding their customers personally and meeting their needs without being asked, promoting the concept of quiet reliability yet available when needed.

The number of touchpoints a company has with its customers is a critical factor in determining its success in the digital economy. Customers of the most successful companies are contacted at least 10 times a year. Consistent, undisrupted, and the ability to 'serve without being asked' have become the watchwords for digitalized enterprises.
